# Moderator Session Script — Joint Journey Tester

A simple guide for running one ~20-minute session (in person, on a video call, or even by phone while they share their screen). Keep it relaxed and friendly. **You are testing the app, not the person** — say so.

---

## 0. Before the session
- Have the **form SHARE link** ready to send them at the end.
- Have a notepad (or the **Observation sheet** below) open.
- If recording, ask permission first.

## 1. Welcome (1 min) — say something like:
> "Thanks so much for helping. This is an early version of an app for people waiting for hip or knee replacement surgery. There are **no wrong answers** — anything that confuses you is a problem with *our* design, not you. Please **think out loud** as you go: tell me what you're looking at, what you expect, and anything that puzzles you."

Confirm: *"Happy to carry on? You can stop any time."*

## 2. The task — explore for ~5 minutes (silent-ish observation)
Ask them to:
> "Go to **jointjourney.org**, create an account, and just have a look around for about five minutes — try the exercises, the food/weight part, and the getting-ready section."

**Your job: watch and stay quiet.** Don't help unless they're truly stuck. Note:
- Where do they **hesitate**, sigh, squint, or tap the wrong thing?
- What do they **say out loud**?
- Anything they **can't find** or **misunderstand**?
- Only if stuck >30s: *"What were you expecting to happen?"* (then, if needed, help — and note it).

## 3. A few spoken questions (3–4 min)
Light, open prompts before the form:
- "What did you think that was all about, in your own words?"
- "What stood out as useful? Anything pointless or confusing?"
- "If you were waiting for surgery, would you actually use this? Why / why not?"
- "Was anything hard to read or tap?"

## 4. The form (8–10 min)
> "Last bit — could you fill in this short form? It's the same sort of questions, and it gives us a score we can track as we improve. Be as honest as you like — I won't be offended!"

Send the link. Let them complete it **without you reading over their shoulder** (more honest answers). Offer to read questions aloud if they prefer.

## 5. Close (1 min)
> "That's incredibly helpful — thank you. Is there one thing you'd change first?"

Thank them. If you promised an incentive (e.g. a £10 voucher), arrange it.

## After all sessions — pulling it together
1. Average the **SUS score** (see `README.md` for the formula).
2. List every **confusion point**; tally how many testers hit each one → that's your **fix priority order**.
3. Pull the **value / credibility / would-follow** averages from the form.
4. Collect **quotes you have permission to use**.
5. Write 5 lines: *what's working, the top 3 things to fix, and whether interest/credibility looks strong enough to keep going.*
