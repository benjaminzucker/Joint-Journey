# Joint Journey — Advisory Board outreach messages

Ready-to-send drafts. Replace [brackets]. Keep the ask **specific and bounded** —
clinicians say yes to clear scope + purpose, not vague "join my startup."

---

## 1. Psychologist (warm — your friend)
*Tone: informal. Channel: WhatsApp / text / email.*

> Hi [Name]! Bit of a random one — you know I've talked about building something
> for people stuck on NHS joint-replacement waiting lists. I've actually built it:
> **Joint Journey**, a digital prehab programme — physio, weight management, and
> crucially **mental-health / pain-preparation** for the wait and the surgery.
>
> That last pillar is right up your street, and I'd love you to be our **founding
> psychology advisor**. Honestly it's not a big time commitment — reviewing the
> mental-health content to make sure it's safe and sensible, helping flag anything
> we should add, and a quick catch-up each quarter. I'd put it on a proper footing
> (a short advisor agreement, and we can sort honoraria or a small equity stake
> once the company's set up).
>
> Would you be up for a coffee/call so I can show you what I've built and what I'd
> be asking? No pressure at all — but you'd be brilliant for it.

## 2. MSK / orthopaedic physiotherapist
*Two versions — (a) ask a surgical colleague for an intro, (b) direct approach.*

### 2a. Asking a colleague for an introduction (fastest route)
> Hi [Colleague], quick favour — I'm developing a digital **prehabilitation**
> programme for patients on the hip/knee replacement waiting list (exercise +
> weight + mental prep). I'm looking for an **MSK/orthopaedic physiotherapist** to
> advise on and sign off the exercise content. Who's your go-to physio for pre/post-op
> rehab — anyone you'd recommend I approach? A quick intro would be hugely appreciated.

### 2b. Direct approach to a physiotherapist
> **Subject:** Physiotherapy advisor — digital prehab for joint replacement
>
> Dear [Name],
>
> I'm an orthopaedic surgeon developing **Joint Journey**, a digital
> prehabilitation programme for adults awaiting hip or knee replacement. The aim is
> to use the long NHS wait productively — progressive strengthening and range-of-
> movement work, weight management, and mental/pain preparation — so patients go
> into surgery fitter and recover better.
>
> I'm looking for an **HCPC-registered MSK/orthopaedic physiotherapist** to join our
> small clinical advisory board to review and sign off the **exercise content** and
> help with clinical safety. It's a bounded commitment — an initial content review,
> then roughly 2–4 hours a quarter. I'd set it up properly with an advisor
> agreement, and we can discuss honoraria and/or a small equity stake.
>
> Would you be open to a short call to see the product and discuss? Your expertise
> would directly shape something I hope will help a lot of patients.
>
> Best wishes,
> [Mr Benjamin Zucker] · [role] · [contact]

## 3. Registered Dietitian (freelance)
*Channel: email, e.g. via the BDA freelance directory.*

> **Subject:** Dietetic advisor — digital prehab for joint replacement patients
>
> Dear [Name],
>
> I'm an orthopaedic surgeon developing **Joint Journey**, a digital
> prehabilitation programme for adults awaiting hip or knee replacement. Alongside
> physiotherapy and mental-health preparation, it includes a **weight-management /
> nutrition** component, and I'm looking for a **registered dietitian (HCPC)** to
> review and sign off that content and keep it safe and evidence-based.
>
> It's a bounded role: an initial content review, then occasional input (≈2–4 hours
> a quarter) as the programme develops. I'd engage you formally — either a fee per
> review/honorarium, or a small equity stake once the company is incorporated
> (happy to discuss what suits you).
>
> Would you be open to a brief call to see the content and discuss? I'd value your
> expertise in making the nutrition side genuinely helpful and safe.
>
> Best wishes,
> [Mr Benjamin Zucker] · [role] · [contact]

---

### Tips
- Lead with **purpose** (useful use of waiting-list time) and **clear scope**.
- Be explicit it's **advisory** (review/sign-off), not delivering individual care.
- Mention the **advisor agreement** up front — it signals professionalism.
- For the physio, the **colleague intro (2a)** will usually beat a cold email.
