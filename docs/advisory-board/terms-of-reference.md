## 1. Purpose
The Clinical Advisory Board ("the Board") provides independent, multidisciplinary
clinical input to Joint Journey, a digital prehabilitation programme for adults
awaiting hip or knee replacement. The Board exists to:
1. **Assure clinical content** — ensure the exercise, nutrition and
   mental-health/pain-preparation content is safe, evidence-based and appropriate.
2. **Support clinical risk management** — contribute to hazard identification and
   mitigation (feeding the DCB0129 clinical safety case).
3. **Guide claims** — advise on what can and cannot be claimed for the product.
4. **Lend credibility** — provide named clinical oversight for users, NHS trusts
   and funders.

The Board is **advisory**: it informs decisions but does not direct the company.
Final decisions rest with the company/founder, except that **clinical content
must not be published without the relevant advisor's documented sign-off.**

## 3. Roles & responsibilities
**Members will:**
- Review the content for their pillar (initial and on material change) and record
  sign-off in `content-review-and-signoff-log.docx`.
- Help identify hazards and mitigations in their domain (`../clinical-safety/hazard-log.md`).
- Declare conflicts of interest (`conflict-of-interest-register.md`).
- Attend meetings (or send comments) and act within their professional scope.

**Members will NOT:**
- Take responsibility for company decisions or operations.
- Provide individual clinical care to users through this role.

## 4. Independence & conflicts of interest
- Members should be **independent of any NHS organisation that may later
  commission or purchase Joint Journey**, to avoid procurement conflicts. Where a
  member has such a connection, it must be declared and managed (see CoI policy).
- Conflicts are declared on joining, recorded in the register, and revisited at
  every meeting.

## 5. Meetings
- **Frequency:** [quarterly] (≈4/year), plus async reviews as content is developed.
- **Quorum:** the Chair plus at least **[2]** members; the relevant pillar's
  advisor must be present (or have commented) for any decision on that pillar.
- **Minutes:** taken for every meeting (`meeting-minutes-template.md`), recording
  attendance, decisions, sign-offs, actions and declared interests.
- **Decisions:** by consensus; the Chair holds a casting view on non-clinical
  matters. Clinical content sign-off rests with the relevant registered advisor.

## 6. Compensation
- As set out in each member's `advisor-agreement-template.md`.
- Default approach: **honoraria and/or pro-bono initially; equity (FAST-style
  vesting) once the company is incorporated.** [Confirm per member.]

## 7. Confidentiality & IP
- Members keep company information confidential (see advisor agreement).
- Intellectual property in content/advice created for the company is assigned to
  the company (see advisor agreement).

## 8. Indemnity & responsibility
- Members provide advice in good faith within their professional competence.
- The **company (via the CSO)** holds responsibility for the product and its
  deployment. Members should confirm their advisory activity is covered by their
  professional indemnity, or rely on the company's arrangements as agreed.

## 9. Review
- This ToR is reviewed **annually** and updated as the product and regulatory
  position evolve (e.g. at MHRA/medical-device decision points).
