# Joint Journey — Competitor Analysis & Market Positioning

*Last updated: June 2026. This is a living document — update as we learn more (especially pricing, regulatory status, and any published evidence from competitors).*

---

## 1. Summary / "so what"

There are two notable UK digital prehabilitation players: **QuestPrehab** and **PreActiv**. Neither is yet commercially successful (per Companies House filings), neither has locked up the market, and the category is **early, validated, but commercially unproven**.

**Our conclusion:** the market is open for a **focused, low-cost, evidence-building hip/knee prehab product**. We should *not* try to out-concierge QuestPrehab or out-platform PreActiv. We win by being **specific (hip/knee), affordable, accessible (older/less tech-confident patients), and evidence-led via a free NHS pilot** — while running a **consumer/self-referral channel in parallel** to earn revenue and usage data.

## 2. The competitors

### QuestPrehab (questprehab.com)
- **What it is:** "A fully digital prehabilitation platform that delivers personalised, data-driven support to help patients prepare mentally and physically for **major surgery or cancer treatment**."
- **Model:** **High-touch** — personalised and *prescribed*, with a **specialist onboarding session and follow-up**.
- **Scope:** Broad — major surgery **and** cancer.
- **Evidence:** Claims its service is **"proven, published, and successfully used in the UK NHS,"** citing faster recovery and improved health-related quality of life. *(Strongest evidence position of the three.)*
- **Profile:** "As featured in" NHSx, Bowel Cancer UK, BBC Radio 5 Live, Business Mondays. A digitalhealth.net article describes a **King's College Hospital NHS Trust year-long pilot** using QuestPrehab for ~100 hip/knee patients (exercise, nutrition, psychological support).
- **Commercials:** Per Companies House, **not yet profitable / not near making money.**

> **Read:** Their specialist-led model has a **high marginal cost per patient**. That makes them a strong fit for the **private / self-pay / insurer / cancer** market (where patients pay a premium for concierge-style support), and a **poor structural fit for NHS-scale** prehab. Their evidence base is their real moat.

### PreActiv (preactiv.co.uk)
- **Tagline:** "Prepare to Repair — dynamic, individualised perihabilitation."
- **What it is:** An app + tablet platform that "optimises patients before surgery using technology to create individualised prehabilitation programmes."
- **Model:** **Scalable, self-guided digital** — shown white-labelled with **NHS hospital branding**, a patient dashboard ("24 days until your surgery"), modules like "Cardio Fit," and progress tracking.
- **Scope:** **Whole perioperative journey — Prehab → Treatment → Rehab** (incl. post-op rehab); many surgery types.
- **Stated selling points:** "Highly cost effective," "Built to surgery and treatment dates," "Individualised, adaptive and multimodal," "Low carbon footprint," "Reducing health inequalities," "Integrates with major patient-engagement/digital perioperative platforms," "Collaborates with existing perihabilitation services."
- **Evidence:** Currently running a **study in Bath**, but **no published evidence yet.**
- **Commercials:** Per Companies House, **not yet profitable / not near making money.**

> **Read:** This is our **more direct B2B/NHS competitor** — same low-cost, scalable, white-label space. But it appears **unfocused** (all surgeries, whole journey, "integrates with everything") and **has not yet built an evidence moat.** Both are weaknesses we can exploit with focus and proof.

## 4. Our positioning statement

> **Joint Journey is the focused, affordable, easy-to-use prehabilitation programme for people awaiting hip or knee replacement — combining physiotherapy, weight management, and mental/pain preparation in one simple journey, designed for real (often older) patients, and built to generate the NHS evidence that proves it works.**

**Three differentiators to lean on:**
1. **Focus** — *the* dedicated hip/knee product, not a general perioperative platform. Depth and tailoring beat breadth for this specific patient.
2. **Cost + simplicity** — undercut QuestPrehab on price; out-simplify PreActiv for **older, less tech-confident** patients (accessibility is a genuine asset).
3. **Evidence + trust via a free NHS pilot** — the one thing capital can't easily buy, and the gap both competitors leave open.

## 5. Go-to-market sequence

**Run two channels in parallel:**

**A. Consumer / self-referral (now → ongoing)**
- Serve the self-referred patient on a long wait who is in *no* pilot — currently unserved by either competitor.
- Earns early revenue and, crucially, **real usage + adherence + outcome data**.
- Low cost, fully in our control, no governance gatekeepers.

**B. Free NHS trust pilot (the evidence wedge)**
1. **Find a clinical champion** — an orthopaedic surgeon or physio lead who *wants* this and will sponsor it through governance. *(This is the real first milestone — not the product.)*
2. **Clear the governance gate** — finish **DTAC, DPIA, and Clinical Safety (DCB0129)**. These are our moat-in-progress; competitors had to do the same.
3. **Choose the framing deliberately:**
   - **Service evaluation** = fast, no full HRA/ethics, good for adherence + PROM trends + testimonials. *Start here to get moving.*
   - **Research study** (HRA/ethics) = slower but produces **publishable, credible evidence** — the kind that answers QuestPrehab's published-evidence advantage and wins future B2B deals. *Plan this as step 2.*
4. **Measure real outcomes from day one** — pre/post **Oxford Hip/Knee Scores** (already in the app), weight, and a wellbeing measure — so even an informal pilot yields a credible before/after story.
5. **Decide who pays after "free"** *now*, and design the pilot to prove that case: trust budget, per-patient tariff, or consumer/private alongside. Free-forever is not a business.

## 6. Honest risks & open questions

**Risks**
- For B2B, we'd be pitching pre-revenue and pre-evidence against PreActiv (cost-effective, integrated) and QuestPrehab (published outcomes). **Winning B2B early is hard without evidence** → hence consumer-first + pilot.
- A "free" pilot still costs the trust **staff time + IG + clinical sign-off**; without a champion it stalls.
- A quick **service evaluation will not, by itself, produce publication-grade proof.**
- Competitors are weak *now* — but both could raise funding. **Speed matters.**
