How to Use Joint Journey

Everything you need to know to get the most from this programme.

🏔️ The idea is simple: The NHS waiting list for joint replacement surgery is long. Rather than spending that time in pain and frustration, let's use it to get you as strong, healthy, and prepared as possible - so you go into surgery in the best shape you can, and come out the other side faster.

What Is Prehabilitation?

Prehabilitation ("prehab") means preparing your body and mind before surgery. Research consistently shows that people who do prehab before joint replacement surgery:

  • Recover faster after surgery
  • Have shorter hospital stays
  • Experience less post-operative pain
  • Return to normal activities sooner
  • Feel more confident and less anxious going into surgery

Joint Journey brings all of this together in one place - free of charge.

The Four Pillars

Joint Journey is built around four pillars, each designed to prepare you in a different way:

💪

1. Exercise

A 12-week progressive exercise programme designed specifically for people awaiting hip or knee replacement. Starts gentle and builds gradually. Every exercise has a video demonstration.

Aim: 3-4 sessions per week, about 25 minutes each.

🍳

2. Nutrition

Simple, practical nutrition guidance with 30+ recipes, a calorie calculator, weight tracker, and shopping list builder. Focused on healthy eating and optimising your weight for surgery.

Aim: Follow the nutritional guidance at your own pace. Small changes add up.

💚

3. Mindset

Eight evidence-based modules covering pain management, anxiety, sleep, motivation, mental rehearsal, and mental preparation for surgery. Based on cognitive behavioural and visualisation techniques that genuinely work.

Aim: Work through one module per week, or at your own pace.

🏥

4. Getting Ready

Practical preparation guides: what to pack for hospital, how to prepare your home, questions for your surgeon, what happens on the day, and joint-specific precautions.

Aim: Read through these in the weeks before your surgery date.

How Often Should I Use It?

Here's what we'd suggest as a weekly routine:

Activity How often Time
💪 Exercise session 5-6 times per week (with 1-2 rest days) ~25 minutes
💚 Daily mood check-in Daily 30 seconds
⚖️ Weight log 2-3 times per week 1 minute
📚 Mindset module 2 per week 10-15 minutes
🍳 Try a new recipe 3-4 per week Varies

Don't worry about being perfect. Some weeks you'll do loads, other weeks life gets in the way. The important thing is to keep coming back. Even doing a few exercises is better than doing nothing.

The Oxford Score - Measuring Your Progress

When you first start Joint Journey, we'll ask you to complete the Oxford Score - a set of 12 questions used by surgeons across the NHS to measure how your hip or knee affects your daily life.

This gives us a baseline score. Then, 6 months after your surgery, we'll email you a link to complete it again. This lets us - and you - see exactly how much your joint function has improved.

The Oxford Score is scored from 0 (severe problems) to 48 (no problems). Most people see a dramatic improvement after surgery, and we want to capture that for you.

A Note on Safety

Joint Journey is designed to be safe for most people awaiting joint replacement surgery. However:

  • This is not a substitute for medical advice - always follow your surgeon's and physiotherapist's instructions
  • Stop if something hurts sharply - mild discomfort during exercise is normal, but sharp pain is not
  • Talk to your GP before starting the exercises if you have heart problems, respiratory conditions, or balance issues
  • Read our full Medical Disclaimer for more details

A Bit About Our Service

Joint Journey was created because of a simple frustration: hundreds of thousands of people in the UK wait months - sometimes over a year - for joint replacement surgery, and that time is largely wasted. There's strong evidence that prehabilitation improves outcomes, but there's no widely available digital programme to deliver it.

We built Joint Journey to fill that gap. The exercise content is based on published clinical guidelines from NICE, the Chartered Society of Physiotherapy, and peer-reviewed research. The nutritional guidance follows NHS and British Dietetic Association recommendations. The mindset content is based on evidence-based cognitive behavioural approaches.

Read more on our About page.

Tips for Getting the Most Out of It

  1. Set a routine - try to do your exercises at the same time each day (morning is often easiest)
  2. Tell someone - let a family member or friend know you're doing this. Accountability helps.
  3. Track your progress - tick off exercises, log your weight, check your mood. Seeing progress is motivating.
  4. Be patient with yourself - you're in pain, you're waiting for surgery, some days will be hard. That's normal.
  5. Read the mindset modules - they're often the bit people skip, but they might be the most valuable part.
  6. Try the recipes - they're really nice, easy to make, quick, and cheap. Good nutrition genuinely helps with recovery too.
