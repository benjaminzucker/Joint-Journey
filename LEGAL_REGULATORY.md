## 1. MHRA / Medical Device (Software as a Medical Device, "SaMD")
**Why it matters:** UK software can be a regulated medical device if it's
intended for diagnosis, prevention, monitoring, treatment, or alleviation
of disease. Getting this wrong = operating an unregistered device.

- [ ] Get an early opinion (regulatory consultant or MHRA guidance/innovation
      route) on whether Joint Journey is a device or wellness/education.
- [ ] Document the conclusion and the reasoning (keep evidence).

**Our position / arguments for "wellness & education":**
- Generic exercise programmes, recipes, and educational content.
- Not diagnosing, not monitoring a condition, not prescribing treatment.

**⚠️ Risk flags to review (the personalisation edge):**
- Programme level assigned from the Oxford Score.
- Dynamic programme length based on surgery date.
- Adaptive dashboard nudges.
- These tailor content to the individual — confirm this does NOT cross into
  "software making a clinical judgement for a specific patient."

## 2. Indemnity & Insurance
**Why it matters:** Your clinical indemnity almost certainly does not cover
a commercial digital product published to the public.

- [ ] Ask MDU/MPS/MDDUS (and NHS/Trust cover) IN WRITING whether this is
      covered. **Assume NOT until confirmed.**
- [ ] Arrange **Public / Product liability** insurance (harm-claim cover).
- [ ] Arrange **Professional indemnity** for the business.
- [ ] Arrange **Cyber / data liability** (we hold health data).
- [ ] Timing: lower risk while free + tiny userbase; becomes important the
      moment patients act on it, and essential before charging.

---

## 3. GMC / Doctor Dimension
**Why it matters:** As a registered surgeon, content you publish carries
extra weight and professional duty.

- [ ] Notify/seek guidance from your medical defence organisation about
      running a health product as a doctor.
- [ ] Ensure all clinical claims are evidence-based and not misleading.
- [ ] Keep the "not a substitute for medical advice" messaging prominent.

---

## 4. ICO / Data Protection (UK GDPR)
**Why it matters:** We process special-category (health) data.

- [ ] Register with the ICO and pay the data protection fee.
- [ ] Confirm lawful basis + explicit consent for health data
      (consent checkbox exists — get it reviewed).
- [ ] Complete a **Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA)**.
- [ ] Have a privacy/data-protection specialist review the Privacy Policy
      and consent flow.
- [ ] Confirm data storage/processing locations + processor terms
      (Firebase/Google, Netlify) are documented.

---

## 5. Company Setup & Filings
**Why it matters:** A limited company gives a liability shield (separates
the product/business from your personal assets).

- [ ] Form a limited company (Companies House, ~£50 online).
- [ ] Check NHS contract re: declaring outside commercial interests /
      directorship.
- [ ] Set up a business bank account.

**Ongoing obligations (light while pre-revenue):**
- [ ] Confirmation Statement — annually, ~£34 (10-minute job).
- [ ] Annual Accounts — micro-entity/dormant accounts are heavily
      simplified (publicly visible on Companies House).
- [ ] Company Tax Return (CT600) + Corporation Tax — no profit = no tax,
      but usually still file. Tell HMRC if genuinely dormant.
- [ ] PAYE — only if running payroll (avoidable early).
- [ ] VAT — only required above £90k turnover (ignore for now).
- [ ] Engage a small-business accountant once revenue starts
      (~£60–150/mo, or a few hundred/yr for a simple company).

## 6. Commercial Legal (general solicitor)
- [ ] Review **Terms of Service + medical disclaimer** (drafts exist) —
      especially liability limitation.
- [ ] Confirm **IP ownership** of all content/code sits with the company.
- [ ] Cookie/analytics compliance (Google Analytics consent).
- [ ] Later: B2B / NHS / ICB contracts.
- [ ] Later: investment / shareholder paperwork.
